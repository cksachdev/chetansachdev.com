    <h2 style="margin-bottom:1rem;font-size:1.25rem;font-weight:600;color:var(--color-text);">eLearning Developer</h2>
    <p style="margin-bottom:1rem;line-height:1.8;color:var(--color-text);">I'm Chetan Sachdev, an eLearning developer based in Bangalore with a decade of experience building scalable, accessible digital learning solutions for global organisations.</p>
    <p style="margin-bottom:1rem;line-height:1.8;color:var(--color-text);">My core stack spans the full eLearning delivery chain — from authoring standards like <strong>SCORM 1.2 / 2004</strong> and <strong>xAPI (Tin Can)</strong> to open-source frameworks including the <strong>Adapt Framework</strong> for responsive HTML5 courseware and <strong>Sunbird</strong> for large-scale learning platforms. I work closely with LMS administrators and instructional designers to make sure content doesn't just launch — it tracks, reports, and improves.</p>
    <p style="margin-bottom:1rem;line-height:1.8;color:var(--color-text);">Beyond standards and frameworks, I enjoy the intersection of JavaScript engineering and learning design — writing custom Adapt plugins, configuring xAPI statement templates, or wiring up a Sunbird instance for a government skilling programme.</p>
    <p style="margin-bottom:1.5rem;line-height:1.8;color:var(--color-text);">This blog is where I document the things I figure out along the way: server setups, LMS quirks, tooling experiments, and the occasional deep dive into accessibility in courseware.</p>
    <h3 style="margin-bottom:0.5rem;font-size:1rem;font-weight:600;color:var(--color-text);">Focus areas</h3>
    <ul style="margin-left:1.25rem;line-height:2.2;color:var(--color-text-secondary);">
      <li>SCORM 1.2 &amp; 2004 — packaging, debugging, LMS compatibility</li>
      <li>xAPI / Tin Can — statement design and LRS integration</li>
      <li>Adapt Framework — course development &amp; custom plugin authoring</li>
      <li>Sunbird — platform setup, configuration &amp; customisation</li>
      <li>LMS administration (Moodle, TalentLMS, and others)</li>
      <li>Accessible HTML5 courseware (WCAG 2.1)</li>
    </ul>
